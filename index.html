Campaign Notes & Context
⚠️
Context: Spousal Sponsorship Done Right — Initial CPL Spike Explained

The CPL for the "Spousal Sponsorship Done Right" ad initially appeared higher because, at the time of campaign launch, this was the only ad that had been approved and started delivering immediately within the CBO campaign. The remaining ads were still under review and had not yet become active, so all the early spend and lead generation were concentrated on this single ad for the first few hours. As a result, the first lead reflected a temporarily inflated CPL of $16.63.

Once we identified that the other ads had not gone live, we immediately pushed them to active status. After the full ad set began delivering collectively, the CPL for this ad normalized and is no longer showing unusually high acquisition costs. At this stage, the ad is not considered alarming or underperforming from a cost perspective — although comparatively it is currently on the lower end in terms of overall performance versus the stronger-performing creatives in the campaign.

✅
Lead Tracking & Qualification — Attorney Fuel Update

As per the latest report, we have received 26 leads so far until yesterday, and all leads have been properly mapped into Attorney Fuel. These can be viewed under the Qualified Leads section in Fuelytics.

The leads were generated through Meta instant forms, where we included a few qualifying questions to better assess intent and relevance. The form specifically asked applicants:

  • What type of immigration support they are looking for (spouse sponsorship or common-law sponsorship)
  • Whether the spouse or partner is currently inside or outside Canada
  • Whether the sponsorship process has already started — with options: Planning to start soon, Already started, Yes, or Not sure

Since users are consciously completing these qualification questions before submitting the form, the leads appear relevant and reasonably qualified based on the information provided.

At this stage, all captured leads are being properly tracked and mapped into Attorney Fuel, and the campaign flow is functioning as expected.
